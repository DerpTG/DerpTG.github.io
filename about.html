About

A bit about how I got here, how I approach the work, and what I do outside of it.

Who I am

I came into college as a commuter with a head start and a plan to graduate in three years. That meant six classes a semester, a mix of in-person and virtual sections pulled from across the whole Penn State system, and a lot of self-direction. It also meant learning to work with professors and students from most of the PSU campuses, and getting comfortable teaching myself the things a schedule that compressed couldn’t always cover.

I didn’t start out set on security. My early interest was broader computer science, and cybersecurity is where it settled once I found the part of the field that actually pulled me in: figuring out what happened, where systems break, and how to protect the data that matters. On my professors’ advice I spent my summers earning certifications alongside coursework, which is how I picked up Security+ and the Microsoft fundamentals, and it’s the same habit that has me working through CySA+ now.

Alongside school I’ve spent about three years growing into a cybersecurity role at an EdTech startup, starting as an unpaid intern balancing a part-time job and turning it into a paid specialist position. The work has been the governance and communication side of security: privacy compliance, control and tool assessments, and the documentation and training that keep a real platform accountable. It taught me that security is as much a people-and-process problem as a technical one.

The thread through all of it is a methodology more than a checklist: learn something, use it, then relearn it when the ground shifts, because in this field it always does. This site is where I keep that in the open. Everything on it is real work, documented so you can look at how I actually think.
