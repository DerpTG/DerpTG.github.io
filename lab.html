LAB LOG ACTIVE · UPDATED WEEKLY
Standing up a small AD domain to practice attack paths and then detect them. The goal is to map the same lateral-movement chain I reconstructed forensically in my capstone — but from the defender's console this time, watching the telemetry as it happens instead of reading it after the fact.
Wrote custom IDS rules against a malware traffic capture, then tuned them down from a wall of alerts to a handful of real ones. The lesson wasn't writing the rule — it was learning how much of detection engineering is noise reduction, and how a rule that fires on everything is the same as a rule that fires on nothing.
Ingested Sysmon data into Splunk and wrote SPL to surface parent/child process anomalies. Caught a simulated PowerShell spawn from a Word document — the classic macro chain — and turned it into a repeatable saved search. This is the day-to-day muscle a SOC runs on, so it's where I'm spending the most reps.

SIEM triage

The Tier-1 SOC job, in miniature: a queue of alerts, each needing a call. Escalate, investigate, or close — then see whether you matched the right disposition and why. The hard part isn't the obvious true positive; it's the noisy alert that looks scary and isn't, and the quiet one that looks routine and isn't.
